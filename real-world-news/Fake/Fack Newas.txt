Global Summit Announces “72-Hour Work-Free Week” Pilot Program

Doha — In a surprising announcement at the International Future of Work Summit, several countries reportedly agreed to begin testing a new “72-hour work-free week” policy starting next year.

According to summit organizers, the experimental policy would allow employees to work only three days per week while receiving full salaries. Supporters claim the program could improve mental health, increase productivity, and reduce traffic congestion in major cities.

Officials stated that the pilot program would first launch in selected smart cities across Asia and Europe. Technology companies and public universities are expected to participate in the first phase.

However, economists have raised concerns about possible inflation and labor shortages. Some business leaders also questioned whether small companies could survive under such a system.

Despite criticism, social media users quickly celebrated the news, with hashtags related to the proposal trending worldwide.

Government representatives said a final decision would be announced after a six-month review period.

Scientists Claim Moonlight Can Charge Mobile Phones Without Electricity

Geneva — A research team at the International Energy Innovation Forum has reportedly introduced a new technology that allows smartphones to charge directly from moonlight.

According to the team, the device uses a special “lunar energy panel” placed on the back of the phone, which absorbs reflected moonlight and converts it into battery power overnight.

Researchers claimed that on a full moon night, a standard smartphone could gain up to 35% battery charge without using any cable or charger.

The project has attracted attention from major tech companies, with several manufacturers reportedly interested in testing the technology for future flagship devices.

Some experts, however, remain skeptical, arguing that moonlight is too weak to generate meaningful electrical power for consumer electronics.

Despite doubts, the announcement quickly went viral online, with many users calling it the “end of power banks.”

The research team said public testing may begin next winter in selected European cities.
